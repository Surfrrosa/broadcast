Chronology

How we got here.

2018 to present. Every event sourced.

US-Iran tensions span decades: the 1953 CIA coup, the 1979 revolution, the Iran-Iraq War, the downing of Iran Air Flight 655 in 1988, cycles of sanctions and proxy conflicts. This timeline begins at the 2018 JCPOA withdrawal, the most direct chain of events leading to the current war.

For broader context: Council on Foreign Relations: US-Iran Relations, 1953-2024

2018
May 8, 2018
US withdraws from the JCPOA

The Trump administration withdraws the United States from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action, the 2015 multilateral nuclear agreement with Iran. At the time of withdrawal, Iran is in compliance with the deal.

JCPOA background · Arms Control Association
November 5, 2018
US reimposes all Iran sanctions

All sanctions eased under the JCPOA are reimposed.

BBC News
2019
May 2019
Iran begins exceeding JCPOA nuclear limits

Iran starts enriching uranium beyond the levels permitted under the JCPOA, stating this is a response to the US withdrawal and reimposition of sanctions.

BBC News
2020
November 27, 2020
Top Iranian nuclear scientist assassinated

Mohsen Fakhrizadeh, head of Iran's nuclear weapons research program, is killed by a remote-controlled machine gun in Absard, east of Tehran. Israeli operatives had reportedly surveilled him for eight months. This follows a series of assassinations of Iranian nuclear scientists in 2010 and 2012, which reportedly ended in 2013 under Obama administration pressure during nuclear negotiations.

New York Times
2021-2023
2021-2023
JCPOA re-entry talks stall

The Biden administration expresses intent to re-enter the JCPOA. After more than two years of intermittent negotiations, no deal is reached. Remaining JCPOA provisions begin to expire in late 2023.

Arms Control Association
2024
April 1, 2024
Israel bombs Iranian embassy complex in Damascus

Israeli strikes hit a consular building in the Iranian embassy complex in Damascus, Syria, killing 16 people including senior IRGC commanders.

CNN
April 13-14, 2024
Iran launches largest attempted drone strike in history against Israel

Iran launches hundreds of drones and missiles at Israel after giving 72 hours' notice to regional countries. Most projectiles are intercepted.

BBC News
July 2024
Israel assassinates Hamas leader in Tehran

Ismail Haniyeh, political leader of Hamas, is assassinated in Tehran, Iran.

BBC News
September 2024
Israel assassinates Hezbollah leader and IRGC commander in Lebanon

Hassan Nasrallah (Hezbollah) and Abbas Nilforoushan (IRGC) are killed in Israeli strikes in Lebanon.

Reuters
October 1, 2024
Iran launches ~200 ballistic missiles at Israel

Iran launches approximately 200 ballistic missiles at Israel, calling it "self-defense" retaliation for the assassinations.

BBC News
October 26, 2024
Israel strikes Iran

Israel strikes missile and drone manufacturing facilities and air defenses inside Iran. Iran reports 4 soldiers and 1 civilian killed.

New York Times
2025
April 12, 2025
US-Iran indirect nuclear talks begin

First round of US-Iran indirect negotiations held in Muscat, Oman. US Special Envoy Steve Witkoff and Iranian FM Abbas Araghchi communicate through Omani mediators. Five rounds of indirect talks follow.

2025-2026 US-Iran negotiations
June 2025
IAEA: Iran has enough enriched uranium for nine nuclear warheads

The IAEA reports Iran has acquired enough enriched uranium to produce nine nuclear warheads. The White House declares Iran has two months to secure a deal.

IAEA report · Arms Control Association
June 12, 2025
IAEA declares Iran non-compliant

The IAEA Board of Governors passes a resolution (drafted by the US, UK, France, and Germany) declaring Iran non-compliant with nuclear obligations. First such declaration since 2005.

Arms Control Association
June 13, 2025
Israel launches Operation Rising Lion: strikes on Iran

Israel launches surprise strikes on dozens of Iranian nuclear facilities, military bases, and infrastructure. More than 200 fighter jets drop 330+ munitions on approximately 100 targets. At least 14 nuclear scientists are killed. Israel publicly claims responsibility. The US also conducts airstrikes on Iran's nuclear facilities during this conflict.

Twelve-Day War · Britannica
June 15, 2025
Iran and Houthis strike Israel

Iran and Yemen's Houthis launch ballistic missiles at Israel, hitting buildings in Bat Yam, Rehovot, a mall in Kiryat Ekron, and Tel Aviv. Nine killed, approximately 200 injured.

Twelve-Day War
June 24, 2025
Trump declares ceasefire

Trump declares a ceasefire on Truth Social, claiming the US has achieved its objectives of preventing Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon.

Twelve-Day War
August 28, 2025
UK, France, and Germany trigger UN "snapback" sanctions

The E3 triggers the UN snapback mechanism, citing Iran's "significant non-performance" of JCPOA commitments: enrichment beyond civilian needs, lack of transparency, and restricted IAEA access.

Snapback mechanism · UK House of Commons Library
October 18, 2025
Iran terminates the JCPOA

The JCPOA was scheduled to terminate remaining sanctions on this date. Instead, Iran officially announces the termination of the agreement. China, Iran, and Russia challenge the legality of the snapback, arguing the E3 had themselves ceased performing their JCPOA obligations.

JCPOA
December 28, 2025
Mass protests erupt across Iran

Nationwide protests over soaring prices after the rial plunges. Demonstrators demand fundamental political change.

2026 Iran massacres · Amnesty International
2026
January 8, 2026
Iran shuts down internet, opens fire on protesters

Iran shuts down the internet nationwide. Security forces open fire on protesters. Verified footage shows forces shooting from rooftops with rifles and shotguns, targeting heads and torsos.

Death toll figures vary widely:

  • 3,117: Iranian government
  • 6,126: Activist estimates (NPR)
  • 7,007 confirmed, 11,744 under review: HRANA
  • 30,000-36,500: Iran International and other outlets
NPR · Amnesty International · 2026 Iran massacres
January 13, 2026
Trump tells Iranians to "keep protesting"

Trump addresses Iranian protesters: "Keep protesting. Take over your institutions. Help is on its way."

Prelude to the 2026 Iran conflict
January 23, 2026
US announces largest Middle East military buildup since 2003

Trump announces a US "armada" heading to the Middle East, including the USS Abraham Lincoln carrier group and guided-missile destroyers.

2026 US military buildup
Early February 2026
IRGC attempts to seize US tanker in Strait of Hormuz

Six IRGC Navy gunboats attempt to seize the US tanker Stena Imperative in the Strait of Hormuz. The tanker continues under escort of USS McFaul.

Prelude to the 2026 Iran conflict
February 2026
Final US-Iran negotiations fail

Three rounds of indirect nuclear talks fail to produce a diplomatic resolution. Iran wants talks confined to civilian nuclear guarantees. The US demands inclusion of missile programs, regional proxies, and human rights.

2025-2026 negotiations · The Conversation
February 24, 2026
Trump warns US is "prepared to act" in State of the Union

Trump accuses Iran of reviving nuclear weapons efforts and advancing missile capabilities threatening the US, Europe, and US bases.

Prelude to the 2026 Iran conflict
February 26, 2026
Final Geneva negotiations end without breakthrough

High-stakes US-Iran talks in Geneva, mediated by Oman, end without agreement.

2025-2026 negotiations
February 28, 2026
US and Israel launch Operation Epic Fury

US and Israeli forces launch nearly 900 strikes in 12 hours targeting Iranian missiles, air defenses, military infrastructure, and leadership. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei is killed.

2026 Iran war · PBS · CNN
February 28, 2026
Minab school airstrike

A strike hits a girls' elementary school in Minab, killing approximately 165 people, mostly girls aged 7-12. The school was built on a former IRGC base that had been closed 15 years prior. Both the US and Israel initially deny involvement. CNN analysis and NBC reporting later indicate the US was likely responsible.

Human Rights Watch · CNN investigation · Al Jazeera
March 1, 2026
Iran retaliates with hundreds of drones and missiles

Iran launches drones and ballistic missiles at Israel and US military bases in Qatar, Bahrain, UAE, Kuwait, Iraq, Jordan, and Saudi Arabia. At least 10 killed in Israel. A girl is killed in Kuwait from shrapnel.

Al Jazeera · CBS News
March 1, 2026
6 US service members killed in Kuwait

An Iranian drone strikes a makeshift operations center at a port in Kuwait. Six US service members are killed. Their remains are returned to Dover Air Force Base on March 7.

CNN · Washington Post
March 2, 2026
Hezbollah launches missiles at Israel from Lebanon

Following Khamenei's killing, Hezbollah launches strikes on Israel. Israel responds with strikes on Beirut and a ground incursion into southern Lebanon. At least 217 killed and 798 wounded in Israeli attacks across Lebanon through March 8.

2026 Lebanon War · Al Jazeera
March 2, 2026
Iran closes the Strait of Hormuz

Iran's IRGC announces closure of the Strait of Hormuz, threatening any ship that passes through. Tanker traffic drops to approximately zero. 150+ ships anchor outside the strait. 13 million barrels/day (31% of all seaborne crude) normally transit the strait.

2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis · NPR
March 2, 2026
Rubio: US struck because it knew Israel was going to

Secretary of State Rubio states the US knew Israel was going to strike and launched preemptive attacks to protect US forces: "We knew that there was going to be an Israeli action... and we knew that if we didn't preemptively go after them before they launched those attacks, we would suffer higher casualties." Rubio later walks back these statements.

Al Jazeera · Times of Israel · Al Jazeera (walkback)
March 3, 2026
White House offers shifting rationales for the war

The Washington Post reports the administration has offered shifting rationales for the strikes: regime change, preemption, eliminating nuclear and missile programs. The Arms Control Association notes the administration has not provided evidence Iran was close to a nuclear weapon. The IAEA states it has no evidence Iran has a nuclear weapons program.

Washington Post · Arms Control Association · NPR
March 3-4, 2026
Landstuhl suspends labor and delivery ward

Landstuhl Regional Medical Center, the largest US military hospital outside the US, suspends its labor and delivery ward "until further notice," citing the need to prioritize combat casualty care.

Military Times · Stars and Stripes
March 4, 2026
Senate War Powers Resolution fails 53-47

The Senate votes 53-47 against a resolution to constrain the president's war authority. The House follows on March 5, voting 219-212 against.

Washington Post · CNN · CS Monitor
March 4, 2026
NATO intercepts Iranian ballistic missile over Turkey

An Iranian ballistic missile heading toward NATO member Turkey is intercepted. First time NATO territory has received incoming fire from Iran. Both NATO Secretary General Rutte and Defense Secretary Hegseth rule out Article 5 invocation. Iran denies firing the missile toward Turkey.

CNBC · Al Jazeera
March 7, 2026
Treasury Secretary: "Biggest bombing campaign" still to come

Treasury Secretary Bessent states the largest phase of the bombing campaign has not yet occurred.

Euronews
March 8, 2026
7th US service member dies; Pentagon reveals ~140 wounded

Army Sgt. Benjamin Pennington, wounded March 1 at Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, becomes the 7th US combat death. Separately, the Pentagon discloses that approximately 140 service members have been wounded since the war began. Of those, 108 have returned to duty; 8 remain in serious condition.

PBS · CNN
March 9, 2026
Iran names Mojtaba Khamenei as new supreme leader

Mojtaba Khamenei, son of the killed Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, is named Iran's new supreme leader. He launches Iran's first wave of missiles under his command. Oil surges past $120/barrel before falling back. World markets plunge: Nikkei drops 5.2%, South Korea's KOSPI triggers a circuit breaker.

NPR · NPR (markets) · CNBC
March 10, 2026
Hegseth promises "most intense day of strikes"; Trump signals war nearing end

Defense Secretary Hegseth announces March 10 will be the most intense day of strikes inside Iran, claiming 90% of Iran's missile launch capacity has been destroyed. CENTCOM reports 5,000+ targets struck in 10 days. Hours later, Trump signals a "near-complete" diplomatic breakthrough. Iran's IRGC responds: "Iran will determine when the war ends."

NPR · Al Jazeera · CBS News
March 11, 2026
IEA announces largest emergency oil release in history

The International Energy Agency coordinates a 400-million-barrel strategic petroleum reserve release across all 32 member states, the largest in IEA history. The US contributes 172 million barrels from the SPR. Oil prices dip briefly but recover within hours as traders conclude the release cannot offset the loss of Hormuz transit.

IEA · CNBC
March 11, 2026
CENTCOM: AI turning targeting from "hours to seconds"

CENTCOM commander Admiral Brad Cooper states AI systems are being used extensively in Operation Epic Fury, compressing processes that "used to take hours and sometimes even days into seconds." Separately, Airwars and The Independent publish an investigation identifying what they describe as the first confirmed civilian killed in a US AI-assisted strike, involving Palantir's Maven Smart System.

DefenseScoop · Airwars
March 11, 2026
Russia confirmed providing drone tactics to Iran

CNN reports Russia is sharing specific Shahed drone wave-attack strategies from its Ukraine war to help Iran penetrate Gulf state air defenses. The Washington Post had previously reported Russia was sharing US warship and aircraft locations with Iran.

CNN · FDD
March 11, 2026
Senate Democrats launch campaign to force public war hearings

A group led by Senators Schumer, Kaine, Murphy, Duckworth, Baldwin, and Schiff announce plans to file repeated war powers resolutions and threaten to "seize the floor" until the White House agrees to public hearings. They demand Defense Secretary Hegseth and Secretary of State Rubio testify publicly.

NPR · Al Jazeera
March 12, 2026
Iran's new supreme leader vows Strait of Hormuz will remain closed

Mojtaba Khamenei issues his first public statement, declaring the Strait of Hormuz must remain closed "as a tool to pressure the enemy" until the war ends. The statement is read on state television over a still photo. No video or audio of the new leader has been released. Oil prices spike on the announcement: WTI settles at $96, Brent breaks $100 for the first time.

NPR · CNBC
March 12, 2026
6 vessels attacked in Persian Gulf; 1 crew member killed

Six vessels are attacked in the Persian Gulf, up from three the previous day. The US-owned tanker Safesea Vishnu is struck by what Iran calls an "underwater drone attack" near Basra, killing one Indian crew member. A Greek-owned tanker, Zefyros, is also hit. Iran releases video of the attack. At least 19 commercial ships have been damaged since February 28.

Al Jazeera · Lloyd's List
March 12, 2026
Iran strikes Gulf states: Bahrain airport, UAE, Jerusalem

Iran launches a wave of attacks across the region. Fuel tanks near Bahrain International Airport are struck, starting a major fire. A container ship is hit 35 nautical miles from Dubai. One missile strikes within meters of the Old City in Jerusalem, near the Western Wall, Al-Aqsa Mosque, and Church of the Holy Sepulchre. Iran threatens to set regional oil and gas infrastructure "on fire" if its energy sites continue to be targeted.

Al Jazeera
March 12, 2026
Pentagon tells Congress: $11.3 billion spent in first week

The Pentagon briefs lawmakers that Operation Epic Fury cost $11.3 billion in its first six days, acknowledging this omits many war-related expenses. Congressional estimates now place the daily rate at $1.5-2 billion per day, up from the Pentagon's initial $1 billion estimate.

Military Times · Al Jazeera
March 12, 2026
Iran's president sets three conditions to end the war

President Pezeshkian states Iran will end the war only if three conditions are met: recognition of Iran's legitimate rights, payment of reparations, and international guarantees against future aggression. Separately, Iran's Ministry of Intelligence reportedly reached out to the CIA through a third country to discuss terms. Trump responded: "It's too late."

Al Jazeera · Jerusalem Post
March 12, 2026
UNHCR: 3.2 million Iranians internally displaced

The UN refugee agency reports up to 3.2 million Iranians have been temporarily displaced, mostly fleeing Tehran and major cities toward northern provinces and rural areas. Iran also hosts 1.65 million refugees, primarily Afghan, who are also affected. UNHCR's 2026 funding is only 15% met.

UNHCR · TRT World
March 12, 2026
UN Security Council adopts Resolution 2817 condemning Iran's Gulf attacks

The Security Council votes 13-0-2 to condemn Iran's attacks on Gulf states, including the Bahrain airport strike and the missile near Jerusalem's Old City. China and Russia abstain, calling the resolution "extremely unbalanced" for omitting any mention of the US-Israeli strikes that started the war. A Russian alternative resolution calling for an immediate cessation of all hostilities fails 4-2-9.

UN Press · Al Jazeera
March 13, 2026
US KC-135 refueling tanker crashes in western Iraq; all 6 crew killed

A KC-135 Stratotanker collides with another aircraft during a mid-air refueling operation over western Iraq. All six crew members are killed. CENTCOM says the crash was not caused by enemy fire. Islamic Resistance in Iraq claims responsibility. The second aircraft lands safely. Total US service member deaths in the war rise to 13.

NPR · CENTCOM
March 13, 2026
Oil continues to climb: WTI at $99, Brent at $103

Brent crude closes above $100 for the second consecutive day, settling at $103.14. WTI reaches $98.71. Both benchmarks post weekly gains of 8-10%. The S&P 500 falls to a new low for the year, its third straight losing week.

CNBC
March 13, 2026
Mexico, Colombia, and Brazil issue joint ceasefire call

The three largest Latin American economies issue a joint statement calling for an immediate ceasefire and the opening of humanitarian corridors in Iran.

Bloomberg
March 13, 2026
Washington Post poll: public opposition to war narrows

A Washington Post poll finds 42% support and 40% oppose the war, a significant shift from the 56-44 oppose-support split in the first week (NPR/PBS/Marist). However, 65% say the administration has not clearly explained the goals of the war, and 42% say the US should stop strikes at this time.

Washington Post
March 14, 2026
Trump rejects ceasefire calls

President Trump rejects the Latin American joint ceasefire call and a separate UN appeal, stating that the US will continue strikes "until Iran's military capacity is completely destroyed." He calls the ceasefire proposals "weakness." Both Trump and Iran rejected international mediation efforts.

Al-Monitor / Reuters · Times of Israel
March 14, 2026
Iran strikes Saudi and Qatari military infrastructure

Iran launches ballistic missile and drone strikes against military facilities in Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE used to stage US operations. Qatar's Ministry of Defence confirms attacks; five US Air Force refueling planes are damaged at a Saudi airbase. Iran says the strikes target foreign military infrastructure supporting the war.

Al Jazeera
March 14, 2026
31st Marine Expeditionary Unit deploying to Persian Gulf

The 31st MEU, based in Okinawa, and the USS Tripoli are ordered to the Middle East, adding approximately 2,500 Marines and sailors to the theater.

USNI News · Military Times
March 14, 2026
Trump calls for allied warships at Strait of Hormuz

President Trump calls on China, France, Japan, South Korea, and the UK to send warships to help reopen the Strait of Hormuz, which has been effectively closed to commercial tanker traffic since the war began. No government publicly confirmed deployment plans.

Al Jazeera · Fortune
March 14, 2026
NPR two-week accounting: 15,000+ targets, $20B+ spent

NPR publishes a comprehensive accounting of the war's first two weeks. The US-Israeli campaign has struck over 15,000 targets across Iran. Estimated cost to the US exceeds $20 billion. Iranian Red Crescent reports 19,775 homes and 4,511 commercial buildings damaged. Gas has risen 25% to $3.68/gallon nationally.
